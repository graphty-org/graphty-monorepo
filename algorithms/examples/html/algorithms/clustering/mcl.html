MCL (Markov Clustering) Explained

Like water flowing through pipes - clusters form where flow gets trapped!

MCL (Markov Clustering) simulates random walks in the graph. It alternates between expansion (spreading flow) and inflation (concentrating flow), causing clusters to emerge naturally where flow gets stuck.

Key Concept:

MCL works through two alternating steps:

1. Expansion: Spreads flow by taking powers of the transition matrix (like spreading through multiple hops)

2. Inflation: Concentrates flow by taking element-wise powers and normalizing (strong connections get stronger)

This process continues until flow stabilizes, with clusters forming around "attractor" nodes where flow concentrates. It's inspired by how random walks tend to stay within dense communities!
